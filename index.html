Beginner's Prompting Guides


The Art of the Prompt

Learn to write clear, concise, and effective prompts. Be specific, provide context, and state your desired format.

Define a Persona

Assign the AI a role (e.g., "You are a senior software developer") to frame its knowledge and response style.

Provide Rich Context

Give the AI background info, examples, and constraints. The more it knows, the better its output will be.

Iterate and Refine

Your first prompt isn't always your last. Refine your request based on the AI's output to zero in on the perfect result.

Advanced Prompt Engineering

Few-Shot Prompting

Guide the AI by providing a few examples of the input-output format you want. This helps it understand the task better than a zero-shot (no examples) prompt.

Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Encourage the AI to "think step by step" to solve complex problems, leading to more accurate and reasoned answers.

Q: Roger has 5 tennis balls. He buys 2 more cans of tennis balls. Each can has 3. How many does he have now?

A: Let's break this down step-by-step:
1. Roger starts with 5 balls.
2. He buys 2 cans * 3 balls/can = 6 new balls.
3. Total balls = 5 + 6 = 11.
The answer is 11.

Self-Consistency

Generate multiple Chain-of-Thought responses for a complex question and choose the most common answer to improve accuracy and reliability.

Prompt the AI with the same CoT question multiple times. If 3/4 responses give the answer '11' and 1/4 gives '10', you can be more confident that '11' is the correct answer. This mitigates occasional reasoning errors.
